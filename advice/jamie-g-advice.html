Hi, my name is Jamie Glauber and I have some advice for you!

You will be confused at first.

Like anything else you're learning for the very first time, it takes time to become acquainted with something new. Coding is a brand new language you are learning. All of the ins and outs, abbreviations, coder "slang" terms, etc are all foreign at first.

Don't give up. Thousands of people from all walks of life learn to code every day. You can do this.

Be patient with yourself.

The most important advice I can give you is this: pace yourself. You will not learn this in a week, or a month. In order to retain information, you need to give yourself time to process everything you've just learned. Sometimes there would be a particular subject that would confuse me and I would need to take a break and think it over before I was ready to return to the computer and conquer my obstacle.

This is a normal part of the process of learning a new technology, and in fact, it's a critical part of the process.

Remember this- learning to code is well within your reach and you CAN do this!
