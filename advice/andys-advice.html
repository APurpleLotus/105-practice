Hi, my name is Andy, and I have some advice for you!

Take breaks, not vacations!

The biggest challenge I faced was taking too much time off between my lessons. Lets face it, learning the command line can be so discouraging we probably would rather clean the entire house before sitting down to figure out how to deal with misplaced repos or mispellings in our commit messages. That said, my house has been tidier than usual for a while now...

Since I am on my 3rd attempt to power through this GIT section, it felt like I'd already forgotten a lot of html and CSS! I figured this little project would be a great opportunity to dust off some of my previously aquired skills, especially CSS. So as much as I want to half-ass this and move on, I think I need to put some thought into how I'd like to lay things out.

Embrace your personal goals.

Though most of us have similar reasons to learn how to code, the rest of our journey including what's behind the finish-line may be vastly different. Some want to work from home while others may find office spaces more productive. Some may be content just knowing enough to get by while others plan on expanding their knowledge via extracurrucular means. All of these are valid goals!

Knowing what you want to get out of this, it is also important to not mistake your own insecurities as disinterest in certain subject matters. While Skillcrush's Slack channels are useful, the amount of students inquiries tend to drown out topic discussions you may find helpful. It is not a bad idea to reach out to other communities or subscribe to blogs that are beneficial to your own growth!
